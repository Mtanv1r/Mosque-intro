The Mosque



Originally, mosques were simple places of prayer for the early Muslims, and may have been open spaces rather than elaborate buildings.

The Great Mosque of Kairouan in present-day Tunisia was the first mosque built in the Maghreb (northwest Africa), with its present form (dating from the ninth century) serving as a model for other Islamic places of worship in the Maghreb.
